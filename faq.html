Have Questions? Start Here.

1. What is climate change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, caused mainly by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

2. What are the main greenhouse gases?

The major greenhouse gases are carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), nitrous oxide (N₂O), and fluorinated gases. CO₂ is the most widely produced.

3. How do we know climate change is real?

Scientists use satellite data, ice cores, temperature records, and ocean research. The overwhelming scientific consensus is that the Earth is warming and humans are the primary cause.

4. Can an individual really make a difference?

Yes! Personal choices—reducing waste, cutting energy use, choosing sustainable products, and educating others—scale up when millions of people act.

5. What countries contribute the most to climate change?

The largest contributors are countries with high industrial output and energy use, especially the United States, China, India, and countries in the EU.

6. Is it too late to stop climate change?

No. While some effects are irreversible, strong global action can slow warming, protect vulnerable ecosystems, and prevent more severe consequences.

Knowledge is the first step toward action.
