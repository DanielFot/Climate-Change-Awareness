What Is Driving Global Warming?

Human activities have caused the largest increase in atmospheric greenhouse gases in history. The burning of fossil fuels—coal, oil, and gas—releases massive amounts of CO₂. This intensifies the greenhouse effect and traps heat close to Earth's surface.

Main Causes

  • Fossil Fuel Combustion: Responsible for nearly 75% of all emissions.
  • Deforestation: Earth loses 10 million hectares of forest each year.
  • Agriculture: Methane from livestock and rice fields is extremely powerful.
  • Industrial Processes: Cement production alone contributes 8% of global CO₂.
  • Waste Mismanagement: Landfills produce methane as organic waste breaks down.

Other Contributors

Modern civilization also contributes through:

  • Urban expansion and land-use change
  • Energy-intensive manufacturing
  • Plastic pollution releasing toxic chemicals
  • Transportation: cars, planes, ships

Together, we can address the root causes.
