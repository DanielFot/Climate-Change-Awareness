What Is Driving Global Warming?

The primary cause of modern climate change is human activity. The burning of fossil fuels releases vast amounts of carbon dioxide (CO₂) into the atmosphere. This creates the greenhouse effect—heat gets trapped, and the planet warms.

Main Causes

  • Burning Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, and natural gas for electricity, heat, and transportation.
  • Deforestation: Trees store carbon; cutting them down releases it back into the atmosphere.
  • Agriculture: Livestock produces methane, a greenhouse gas 28x more powerful than CO₂.
  • Industrial Processes: Factories emit large quantities of heat-trapping gases.
  • Waste Mismanagement: Landfills produce methane; plastics break into harmful particles.
