The Consequences Are Everywhere

Climate change has already reshaped the planet. Rising temperatures intensify natural disasters, disrupt ecosystems, and threaten human health, food security, and global stability. The effects are no longer predictions—they are happening now.

Major Effects

  • Rising Sea Levels: Ocean levels have risen over 20 cm in the last century, flooding coastal regions.
  • Extreme Weather: Heatwaves, storms, hurricanes, and wildfires are becoming more frequent and severe.
  • Melting Ice Caps: Greenland and Antarctica are losing ice at record speed, accelerating sea level rise.
  • Wildlife Disruption: Animals are losing habitats; 1 in 6 species faces extinction if warming continues.
  • Human Health Risks: Pollution, heat, disease spread, and water scarcity threaten millions.

Impact on Humans

Climate change affects every part of our lives:

  • Loss of homes from hurricanes & floods
  • Increased spread of diseases like malaria
  • Food shortages due to droughts & soil degradation
  • Heat-related illnesses & deaths
  • Economic instability in farming and fishing communities

Impact on Oceans

Oceans absorb 90% of excess heat created by greenhouse gases. This leads to coral bleaching, changes in fish migration, and declining marine biodiversity.

Impact on the Future

If global warming exceeds 2°C, scientists predict irreversible effects such as collapsing ice sheets, cataclysmic storms, and severe ecosystem loss.

Understanding the effects is the first step to action.
